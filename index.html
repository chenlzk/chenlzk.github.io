Short Biography

I am currently working as a Software / Research Engineer at Snap Inc. I got my PhD at Department of Computer Science and Engineering, Arizona State University, under the supervision of Prof. Baoxin Li. My research interest includes computer vision & Graphics, applied machine learning and data mining. Before coming to ASU, I received my B.S. and M.S. degree in 2008 and 2011 from the computer science and technology department, Shandong University.

Selected Projects

View recommendation for 360-degree panorama image
I worked on this project during my 2015 summer research intern in Nokia Technologies. We are working on the problem that how to display the 360 degree panorama image in traditional display devices while at the same time preserving aesthetic visual quality.
Image ranking/classification based on semantic attributes
Images can be described by semantic attributes, e.g., natural, open, etc., which are able to brige the low-level image representation and high-level human understanding. We proposed a set of machine learning algorithm to improve the image classification/ranking performance based on semantic attributes.
Skill evaluation on video-based surgical training
video-based surgical training system has been well adopted to train new surgeons. On this project, we developed a system to analyze surgeons' skill and recommend tutorial video for self-skill improvement.
Geometric texture transfer on model deformation
Geometric texture is usually distored during 3D model deformation, especially when resizing operation invloved. In this project, we implement an algorithm which can keep the appearance of geometric textures during model deformation based on geometric synthesis.
